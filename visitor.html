Everything You Need to Know

Planning your trip to Taniti? We've gathered the most common questions and useful information to help you prepare for your visit - from travel tips and currency to local customs and emergency services.

Frequently Asked Questions

The official language is English, though many locals also speak Tanitian Creole. You'll have no trouble communicating with residents and service staff.

The U.S. dollar (USD) is used throughout Taniti. Major credit cards are accepted in most hotels and restaurants, but bring cash for small shops and markets.

Yes - Taniti is known for its welcoming community and low crime rate. Standard travel precautions are recommended, particularly in busy areas and at night.

Taniti has a modern hospital in the capital and several local clinics. In an emergency, dial 911 for immediate assistance.

Most travelers prefer the dry season from May to October, when temperatures are warm and skies are clear. The rainy season (November-April) still offers beautiful scenery and fewer crowds.

Visitors from most countries, including the U.S., Canada, and EU nations, do not require a visa for stays under 90 days. Always check your country's current travel agreements before departure.
